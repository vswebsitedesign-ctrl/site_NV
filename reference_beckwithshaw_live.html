Brick repointing is essential for maintaining the strength, weather resistance and appearance of brickwork. As mortar joints age, they begin to crack, crumble and fall away, leaving gaps that allow water to penetrate the wall. This can lead to damp problems, frost damage and gradual structural weakening. Our Repointing Process

Our brick repointing process begins with removing the old, weakened mortar from between the brick joints. This is carried out carefully to avoid damaging the surrounding masonry and to ensure a solid base for the new mortar. Once the joints are cleaned and prepared, fresh mortar is applied evenly and finished to match the existing style of the building wherever possible. This creates a strong, weather-resistant bond that restores both function and appearance.

Repointing & Brickwork

Signs Your Brickwork Needs Repointing

Properties that have not been repointed for many years often show visible signs of mortar failure. Crumbling joints, loose material, damp patches and visible gaps between bricks are all signs that repointing may be needed. If these issues are ignored, water can continue to enter the wall, increasing the risk of frost damage, weakened masonry and expensive repairs later. Timely repointing prevents these issues from escalating and helps maintain the condition of the property.
