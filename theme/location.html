Damaged mortar joints are one of the most common causes of water ingress in brick buildings. Even small cracks in pointing can allow moisture to enter, especially during periods of heavy rain or freezing temperatures. Once moisture gets into the wall, it can cause bricks to become unstable, internal damp patches to form and sections of masonry to deteriorate faster. Repointing the brickwork seals those vulnerable joints, helping to prevent further damage and keeping the building protected against the weather.

Repointing & Brickwork

Our Repointing Process

Our brick repointing process begins with removing the old, weakened mortar from between the brick joints. This is carried out carefully to avoid damaging the surrounding masonry and to ensure a solid base for the new mortar. Once the joints are cleaned and prepared, fresh mortar is applied evenly and finished to match the existing style of the building wherever possible. This creates a strong, weather-resistant bond that restores both function and appearance.

Repointing & Brickwork

The Benefits of Repointing

Well-maintained mortar joints do far more than improve appearance. Repointing strengthens the wall, reduces water penetration and helps extend the life of the entire structure. It also improves the visual finish of the property by replacing cracked and uneven joints with clean, consistent pointing. This can make a major difference to the appearance of tired brickwork and can improve kerb appeal significantly.

Repointing & Brickwork

Signs Your Brickwork Needs Repointing

Properties that have not been repointed for many years often show visible signs of mortar failure. Crumbling joints, loose material, damp patches and visible gaps between bricks are all signs that repointing may be needed. If these issues are ignored, water can continue to enter the wall, increasing the risk of frost damage, weakened masonry and expensive repairs later. Timely repointing prevents these issues from escalating and helps maintain the condition of the property.

Preserving Your Brickwork Long-Term
